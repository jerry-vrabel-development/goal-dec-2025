# 200 Companies with Strong DEI Commitments - 2025

## Introduction

This list features companies that have demonstrated strong, public commitments to diversity, equity, and inclusion through their policies, practices, and public statements. Many of these companies faced shareholder proposals in 2025 to eliminate DEI programs and successfully defended their commitments.

**Key Indicators Used:**
- Public DEI commitments maintained in 2025
- Shareholder voting support for DEI programs
- Published diversity reports and metrics
- Pay equity audits and transparency
- Employee Resource Groups (ERGs)
- Representation goals and progress tracking

## How to Use This List

### Research Steps
1. **Visit company DEI pages** - Most have dedicated sections on their corporate websites
2. **Read annual diversity reports** - Many publish detailed metrics
3. **Check recent news** - See if they've maintained commitments amid 2025 pressures
4. **Review Glassdoor** - Filter for diversity-related employee reviews
5. **Look for ERGs** - Active employee resource groups signal genuine commitment

### Red Flags to Watch
- Companies that quietly removed DEI pages in 2024-2025
- Diversity statements with no metrics or goals
- No mention of pay equity audits
- Lack of diverse leadership representation
- Token diversity efforts without systemic change

### Green Flags
- Published diversity data with year-over-year trends
- Board-level diversity oversight
- Pay equity audits showing 99%+ parity
- Active ERGs with executive sponsorship
- Shareholders defending DEI programs
- CEO public statements supporting inclusion

### Application Strategy
- Mention your commitment to inclusive workplaces in cover letters
- Ask about ERGs and diversity initiatives in interviews
- Research the specific team's diversity makeup when possible
- Connect with employees from underrepresented groups on LinkedIn

## Important Context (December 2025)

**The Political Climate:**
In 2025, many companies faced unprecedented pressure to eliminate DEI programs through shareholder proposals, executive orders affecting federal contractors, and public campaigns. The companies on this list either:
- Successfully defended their programs through shareholder votes
- Maintained commitments despite external pressure
- Published renewed commitments to inclusion

**What "Strong DEI" Means:**
- Documented policies and practices, not just statements
- Measurable goals and progress tracking
- Pay equity with published results
- Representation at all levels, including leadership
- Support for employees through ERGs and inclusive benefits
- Accountability through regular reporting

**Limitations of This List:**
- Company practices evolve - always verify current status
- DEI strength varies by location and department within companies
- Employee experiences may differ from corporate statements
- Some companies on this list have faced legitimate criticism
