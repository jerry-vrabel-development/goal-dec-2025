- **Mechanical Engineering** - Hardware, manufacturing

- **Policy & Advocacy** - Navigating regulations
- **Finance & Accounting** - Climate investing, carbon markets

### Career Changers Welcome
- Many climate companies hire for aptitude and mission alignment
- Technical bootcamps exist for climate careers (e.g., Terra.do)
- Consider adjacent roles (marketing, HR, operations) at climate companies
- Volunteer or consult to build climate experience
- Network in climate communities (Slack, meetups)

### Compensation Notes
- Startups: Lower base, higher equity potential
- Growth stage: Competitive tech salaries
- Established renewables: Utility-scale compensation
- Non-profits: Lower but mission-driven
- Impact investing: Finance-level compensation
- Climate software: Comparable to tech industry

## Application Strategy

### Standout in Climate Hiring
1. **Show genuine mission alignment** - Not just "I care about climate"
2. **Understand the technology** - Read company blogs, whitepapers
3. **Quantify impact interest** - Gigatons, percentage reductions matter
4. **Highlight relevant skills** - Even non-climate work transfers
5. **Demonstrate scrappiness** - Climate tech needs doers
6. **Network actively** - Climate community is collaborative

### Red Flags to Watch
- **Greenwashing** - Marketing > actual impact
- **Vaporware** - Announcements without deployed technology
- **Poor unit economics** - Climate solution that can't scale profitably
- **Lack of transparency** - No published impact metrics
- **Founder/leadership issues** - Research team stability
- **Regulatory uncertainty** - Extreme policy dependence

### Green Flags
- **Deployed technology** - Not just pilots
- **Customer traction** - Paying customers, not just grants
- **Credible investors** - Lowercarbon Capital, Breakthrough Energy, etc.
- **Technical team** - Domain expertise in leadership
- **Published impact** - Transparent about tons CO2 avoided
- **Sustainable business model** - Path to profitability clear

## Important Context (2025)

**Investment Climate:**
Despite economic uncertainty, climate tech investment reached $44B in 2024 and growing. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) in US providing hundreds of billions in incentives through 2032.

**Job Growth:**
Clean energy jobs growing 2x faster than overall economy. Solar installer is fastest-growing job in America. Climate tech expected to create millions of jobs by 2030.

**Political Climate:**
Federal support fluctuates with administrations, but state-level action, corporate commitments, and global agreements drive long-term demand. Many climate tech companies less affected by federal changes than perceived.

**Skills Gaps:**
Demand far exceeds supply for skilled climate workers. Career changers welcomed. Technical skills transfer from other industries valued.
