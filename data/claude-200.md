## Research Tips

### Prioritization Criteria
- **Tier 1 (Top 50)**: Companies you'd leave everything for - exceptional culture, comp, tech
- **Tier 2 (Next 75)**: Strong options with specific advantages
- **Tier 3 (Next 75)**: Backup options worth exploring

### Next Steps
1. **Check their careers pages directly** - Don't rely on job boards
2. **Look at their engineering blogs** - Quality = culture indicator
3. **LinkedIn research** - See if they're actively hiring senior web devs
4. **Glassdoor reviews** - Filter for engineering reviews from last 6 months
5. **Check tech stack** - Confirm alignment with your skills
6. **Remote policy** - Verify if important to you

### Application Strategy
- Update LinkedIn profile to "Open to Work" (visible to recruiters only)
- Aim for 10-15 quality applications per week (not 100 spray-and-pray)
- Prioritize companies where you have referrals or connections
- Track everything in a spreadsheet: Company, Date Applied, Contact, Status

### Market Insights (Dec 2025)
- Senior roles are IN DEMAND despite headlines
- AI experience is a major plus (even small projects)
- Remote positions declining but still available in AI/security
- Companies finalizing 2026 budgets NOW = good timing
- Inbound applications have low success rate - get referrals or sourced by recruiters

---

**Note**: This list focuses on companies with strong engineering cultures, actual hiring momentum, and technologies relevant to senior web developers. Many are remote-friendly or have multiple office locations. Always verify current hiring status and role availability before applying.
