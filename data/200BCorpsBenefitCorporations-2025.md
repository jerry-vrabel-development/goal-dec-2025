# 200 B Corps & Benefit Corporations - 2025

## Introduction

B Corporations are companies certified by B Lab to meet high standards of social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency. As of March 2025, there are 9,576 certified B Corps across 102 countries. These companies legally commit to benefiting all stakeholders, not just shareholders.

**What Makes a B Corp:**
- Score 80+ out of 200 on B Impact Assessment
- Legal commitment to stakeholder governance
- Public transparency of impact scores
- Re-certification every 3 years
- Focus on Governance, Workers, Community, Environment, and Customers

## Understanding B Corp Scores

### Scoring Breakdown (out of 200 points)
- **Governance:** Company mission, ethics, accountability
- **Workers:** Compensation, benefits, training, worker ownership
- **Community:** Diversity, job creation, civic engagement, supply chain
- **Environment:** Environmental management, air/climate, water, land/life, closed loop
- **Customers:** Customer stewardship, beneficial products/services

### Top Scores Referenced
- **South Mountain Company:** 183 (highest on our list)
- **Dr. Bronner's:** 206.7 (claimed highest ever, dropped certification 2025)
- **Beneficial State Bank:** 176
- **Minimum to certify:** 80
- **Median score:** 50.9 for non-B Corps

## Why Work for a B Corp?

### Mission Alignment
- Your work contributes to verified social/environmental impact
- Company legally committed to stakeholders beyond shareholders
- Transparent about impact through public B Impact scores

### Culture
- Values-driven workplaces
- Often more progressive benefits
- Community-oriented
- Environmental consciousness
- Social responsibility baked into business model

### Career Growth
- Growing sector (9,576 B Corps globally as of 2025)
- Transferable impact experience
- Network of mission-driven professionals
- Increasingly attractive to top talent

## Important Notes

### Controversies
- **Dr. Bronner's** dropped certification in 2025 citing greenwashing concerns with V7 standards
- Some B Corps later acquired by non-B Corps (Ben & Jerry's by Unilever maintained certification)
- Debate over whether B Corp certification goes far enough
- Criticism of allowing single-use products (Nespresso controversy)

### Verification
- B Corp certification is rigorous but not accredited
- Companies self-report data verified by B Lab
- Re-certification required every 3 years
- Standards evolve (currently using Standards V2.1, V7 in development)

### Finding Jobs
- Check company websites directly
- Monster.com has B Corp job filtering
- Purpose Jobs and similar impact job boards
- Network within B Corp community
- Ask during interviews about B Corp commitment
